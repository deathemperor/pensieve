a steady, almost meditative scroll. The single largest gravity well is

The substantive watching is **football** (17%, mostly Manchester
United and the Premier League), then a more familiar Lộc territory:
**Code & Tech** (4.2%), **Cinema & screenplay analysis** (3.5%),
**Long-form essays** (3.4%), and the curious cluster of
**Stoic / Peterson / Modern Wisdom** content (3.3%).
**Aquariums register at just 2.1%** — the hobby is a known central
interest, but the depth lives in the tank room, not the watch history.

Two surprises the data forces into the open. **First:** the search
history contains "Peppa Pig", "Baby Shark", "Sieu Nhan Co Dong",
"Dave and Ava", "PAW Patrol" — at least **149 kids'-content
searches** in the term. This account is shared with a child, and some
unknown fraction of the watch counts isn't Lộc's at all (the rewatched
PAW Patrol two-hour compilation is a clue). **Second:** YouTube Music
is only **0.9%** of the watching — this is a wand for video,
not for sound. Music goes elsewhere.

The pattern reads as *consumption-as-companionship*: news while working,
football highlights while waiting, animated curios while resting,
podcast clips during transit. Wide scope, narrow loyalties — Manchester
United, Theo t3.gg, Outstanding Screenplays, Hannah Fry's brief science
explainers (eight of them in the rewatch list, more than any other voice).

If the Sorting Hat had to choose: not Ravenclaw alone (too restless to
settle on a single discipline), not Hufflepuff alone (too curious-by-
default to be called loyal), but the seam between them — a watcher who
reads to *belong to many places at once*, with one corner of the basin
reserved for the kid who climbs in and asks for Baby Shark.

## VII. What the Memories Cannot Show

*"The Pensieve has limits. Memory is not the same as truth."*

- **Time horizon**. Google Takeout returned only 169 days for this account.
  Anything before **16 November 2025** is invisible here. "Years at Hogwarts"
  could not be computed; only one term.
- **Skipped within seconds counts the same as watched in full.** A 5-second
  click and a 40-minute video are both one row. Rewatch counts in
  Section II are a stronger signal than raw frequency in Section I.
- **Autoplay pollution.** Many entries near the end of a session are autoplay
  the watcher never asked for. The strong gravity of Section I's #1 channel
  is partly autoplay's doing.
- **Signed-out and incognito viewing is invisible.** Anything watched
  without being logged in does not appear in Takeout.
- **Shared devices.** A TV, a partner's borrowed laptop, a child reaching
  for the iPad — those views are recorded as the account-holder's.
- **Orphaned metadata.** 715 entries arrived from Google as
  bare `Watched <URL>` rows — no title, no channel. The video has likely
  since been deleted, made private, or had its metadata stripped. These
  cannot be themed and inflate the "unsorted" bucket.
- **Theme assignment is heuristic.** Channels are matched by name, then
  titles by keyword. 3,869 watches (38.0%)
  fell through to "unsorted" — mostly orphaned-metadata rows above plus
  one-off short-form curiosities.
- **No watch duration.** Takeout records the timestamp of opening, not
  how long the gaze stayed. Hours-watched cannot be reconstructed.
